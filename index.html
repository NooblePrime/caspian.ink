About Me

When I was young, my family would often make long trips to visit our grandparents. During these trips, I was exposed to a vast variety of music ranging from classical pieces by artists like Tchaikovsky and Mozart to more modern hits such as literally anything Flo Rida or Beyonce had released recently. During these times as a kid, I’d begun to associate certain chord progressions and instruments with certain emotions and memories, although as a kid I had no concept of what a chord really was. During my kindergarten year I was introduced to my first real interaction with music through acoustic guitar lessons. I had grown a massive fascination with The Beatles and The Rolling Stones during this part of my childhood, wanting nothing more than to play their songs on my black yamaha acoustic. My first teacher named Hendrick, was my introduction to guitar, although our lessons only lasted for 5 months or so.

My next guitar teacher, Ed Russel, would become a huge part of my life for nearly the next decade. Gifting me my first electric, a sunburst strat which I still own, he shifted my focus from learning basic chords like E-minor and G to learning bar chords, solo sections and scales for improvised riffs. My years playing the guitar from second grade all the way until grade nine were some of the best. I gained a deep appreciation for those who create and write their own music, something I was absolutely abysmal at. Though I was unable to write for shit, I was able to read tabs and learn songs rather quickly. Eventually I was able to pick up on the widely used techniques and patterns used by rock artists, some of which are still seen in my current digital work.

During grade nine, his commute became a problem, which fueled my more recent fascination with hip-hop instrumentals to turn into more of a passion than an interest. That Christmas I received FL Studio as a gift, instantly getting to work trying to make beats that I thought a certain rapper would write lyrics over. This continued for the next year pretty much nonstop, during which I improved drastically. Around this time, a game called Undertale, which I had been aware of since it was released in 2015, was having a sort of renaissance in the fan community. Lots of young artists around my age were starting to use FL Studio to make their own tracks inspired by the game. Knowing I had to get involved, I downloaded a bunch of soundfonts off various websites and got to work. It became a minor obsession for the next three years, during which I consistently uploaded my music to Soundcloud under the alias “Nox”, growing a follower base and even collaborating with several artists. I even managed to fulfill one of my dreams, being a member of the development team for a well known fangame in the community. Unfortunately, the project was scrapped and completely redone from scratch in mid 2024, landing all my work on the soundtrack into an archive. Despite this, I did it all for my own enjoyment rather than any form of praise.

Finally becoming burned out towards the end of 2023, I shifted to yet another recent fan community for a game called Friday Night Funkin’. Ironically enough, I despised this game and its community when it was released in 2020. It wasn’t until 2022 when I finally got over how much I hated the sound of characters making weird semi-rhythmic noises over perfectly good instrumentals and started to see the effort that was put into these songs. Shoutout to my friend Khaye for getting me into the community, a close friend of mine who I’m still working on a mod for the game with. In the present day, I’m making efforts to switch my main DAW to Logic rather than FL Studio, as it's seen more as an industry standard.

Looking ahead, my main goal is pushing the boundaries of game and film scoring like so many others, while maintaining my unique footprint in my work. I hope to branch out and do more collaborations, as it helps me learn from those who are not only more experienced than me, but have a much different style than I do. In addition, I have recently taken up sound design as a heavy interest, as well as the art of foley. I hope to explore both at potential career options in the future as well.
